  • High School Education (10+2)Focus on Science subjects, especially Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Your performance in these subjects is crucial for entrance exams for medical courses.
  • Entrance ExamsPrepare for and take entrance exams like NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) for admission into undergraduate medical courses like MBBS.
  • MBBS DegreePursue an MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) degree from a recognized medical college. This course typically lasts 5.5 years, including a one-year mandatory internship.
  • InternshipComplete the one-year mandatory internship where you get hands-on experience in different departments of a hospital.
  • Postgraduate StudiesAfter your MBBS, you can opt for postgraduate studies like MD (Doctor of Medicine) or MS (Master of Surgery) in your chosen specialty.
  • Super Specialty CoursesIf you wish to super-specialize in a field, you can pursue courses like DM (Doctorate of Medicine) or MCh (Master of Chirurgiae).
  • Medical Practice or Further StudiesStart your medical practice or you can opt for further studies or research. You may also need to register with the Medical Council of India or State Medical Councils to practice medicine in India.
